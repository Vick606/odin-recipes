Classic Beef Round Steak Stew

Description

This traditional beef stew recipe showcases the rich flavor of boneless beef round steak, resulting in a tender, savory dish perfect for a chilly evening. The combination of spices and slow cooking enhances the natural taste of the beef, creating a hearty stew that's ideal for sharing with family and friends. Enjoy this comforting meal with a side of hot Ugali or a plate of fluffy rice to complement its rich gravy, making every bite a delightful experience. Whether served with Ugali, rice, or simply on its own, this stew offers a fulfilling and nutritious option that's sure to satisfy hunger and warm the soul.

Ingredients

  • 1 (2 kg) boneless beef round steak, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • Kosher salt and cracked black pepper to taste
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon canola oil
  • 3 cups chopped onion
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 2 cups dry red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth, or more as needed
  • Others as needed: chopped carrots, 1 cup fresh peas, chopped rosemary, potatoes, Worcestershire sauce

Steps

  1. Step 1: Season the Beef: Toss the beef cubes in a bowl with a generous amount of kosher salt and cracked black pepper. Dredge the seasoned beef in the all-purpose flour, shaking off any excess.
  2. Step 2: Brown the Beef: Heat the canola oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Add half of the beef cubes and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef to a plate and set aside. Repeat with the remaining beef cubes.
  3. Step 3: Cook the Onions and Garlic: In the same pot, add the chopped onions and cook until softened. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Step 4: Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the dry red wine, scraping the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits. Let the wine reduce by half.
  5. Step 5: Add Spices and Tomato Paste: Stir in the smoked paprika, dried thyme, dried rosemary, herbes de Provence, and tomato paste. Mix well to coat the onions and garlic.
  6. Step 6: Return the Beef: Add the beef back to the pot along with the Worcestershire sauce, bay leaves, and beef broth. Stir well to combine.
  7. Step 7: Add Vegetables: Fold in the carrots, potatoes, and peas. Ensure all the ingredients are submerged in the liquid.
  8. Step 8: Simmer: Cover the pot and place it in the oven at 325°F (163°C). Cook for about 2 hours, or until the beef and vegetables are tender.
  9. Step 9: Adjust Seasoning: Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning with additional salt, pepper, fresh thyme, or fresh rosemary if desired.
  10. Step 10: Serve: Ladle the stew into bowls and serve hot. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired.
